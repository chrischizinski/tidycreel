From R/creel-estimates.R — estimate_effort_sections() structure (reference):
- Takes (design, by_quo, variance_method, conf_level, aggregate_sections, missing_sections)
- Builds registered_sections from design$sections[[section_col]]
- Detects absent sections via setdiff(registered_sections, present_sections)
- Loops over sections, calls rebuild_counts_survey(design, sec) per section
- Appends prop_of_lake_total after loop; appends .lake_total row if aggregate_sections

From R/creel-estimates.R — estimate_catch_rate_sections() structure (reference):
- Takes same signature minus aggregate_sections (rates have no lake total)
- Loops over sections, calls rebuild_interview_survey(design, filtered_interviews) per section
- No prop_of_lake_total, no .lake_total row

CRITICAL DIFFERENCE for product section helpers:
- Must call BOTH rebuild_counts_survey AND rebuild_interview_survey per section
- Must check BOTH counts AND interviews for absent sections
- absent_sections = setdiff(registered_sections, intersect(present_count_sections, present_interview_sections))

From R/creel-estimates-total-catch.R — existing helpers:
- estimate_total_catch_ungrouped(design, variance_method, conf_level) -> new_creel_estimates()

prop_of_lake_total denominator:
  sum(TC_i) from present section estimates — NOT full-design svytotal.

  # and interview-based rate designs is not identified.
  lake_se <- sqrt(sum(present_rows$se^2))
